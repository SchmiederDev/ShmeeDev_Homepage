About the App

.NET-MAUI-App, written in C# using an SQLITE-database

E-Citera is a reference administration software that helps users to keep track of all the literature (books, papers and articles etc.) they read and need for their academic work and private purposes and interests as well of course.

  • E-Citera allows users to create a database in which all of the titles of interest to them are stored according to the needs of complete bibliographical detail needed for academic research - although it is of course possible to keep it as simple as just saving the title of a work for future reference.

  • Furthermore users can not only store titles with detailed bibliographical information they can also assign collections of titles to reference lists related to a specific topic or bibliography for one of their own academic works, papers or books.

  • Finally, E-Citera allows users to convert the bibliographical information of a single title or a whole reference list according to the needs of a specific academic citation style, like Harvard referencing, Chicago- or MLA (Modern Language Association)-style and simply copy the created citation directly into your foot- or end notes or a reference list at the end of one of their own papers without the need of formatting the citation themselves.
    But, they are not bound to widely used citation styles because E-Citera let's them actually create and store as many citation styles as they like. So users could easily adapt to the requirements of a small journal for publishing one of their papers without the need of manually (re-)writing all the titles cited.

D&D 5e Character Sheet

Technologies:

Windows Desktop App (WPF)
Code: C#/XAML

About the App

This is an app for the pen & paper fantasy roleplaying game Dungeons & Dragons. Pen & paper means that everything happening in the game - including results of dice rolls and calculations - would usually be written down on sheets of paper. The digital Character Sheet app virtualizes these processes and automatizes game mechanics. (see below for further details)

Download the App:

Windows (x86) – 64 bit:
D&D Character Sheet 5e
(MAC OS & Linux Versions will follow soon)

Details on what the app does:

The rules for the game are quite complex, it works with differents types of dice, a serious amount of values describing the capabilities of the different types of characters, a lot of items – weapons, armor, tools etc. – and hundreds of spells for some of the character classes.
Usually, you would write them down on sheets of paper, do a lot of calculations based on the values of your character in combination with the dice role results or shuffle through the rule books – e. g. to find the price of an item or the mechanics of a 'spell' (magic some characters are able to do) etc.
This app is designed to make the life of players (users) easier and helps them to focus on having fun because the app does all the calculations automatically and users neither need physical dice nor do they need to know which die to use in which case – because the app does and lets them roll on the interface, already adding and substracting the values for them.
The app also comes with databases for items and spells. Players can comfortably buy items & add them to their inventory from the digital 'merchant-store' as well as equip weapons and armor in the Inventory Window.
You don‘t have to know which spells are available for your character because the app automatically presents a limited selection from the database based on the class of your character.
Finally, you can of course save & load your accomplishments as a player: progress in level & skill, treasure in your inventory as well as notes on the development and experiences of your character and the story in general.

How the game works:

In Dungeons & Dragons every player assumes the role of a character with special skills in a diverse team of adventurers: They specialize e. g. fighting, healing, sneaking or magic. Players work together as a team and their different skills complement each other. The digital Character Sheet app virtualizes these processes and automatizes game mechanics. (see below for further details)
Finally, a special kind of player, the so called 'Game Master', tells the story, describes the environment and assumes the role of all characters other than the players – Non-Player-Character's (NPC's) – as well as the role of the enemies of the players. (She or he does what the code in a computer game would do.)
The band of adventurers (the characters of the players) travel through a fantasy universe – usually a more or less medieval setting – fight monsters or evil persons and organizations and collect treasures along the way.
Last but not least while doing so, players experience complex storylines in which they interact with friendly or hostile NPC's, usually to the end of solving a quest. A quest is a task given to them by one of the NPCs occuring in the game. This can involve all kinds of things, but for example they have to find long lost relatives, set out for a rescue mission or procure a powerful item for somebody hidden in long forgotten ruins or the lair of deadly monster.

Trouble in Andromeda VII game is designed to be a nice little, space shooter of the classic type. A minigame, perfect for playing in between and taking five minutes off: Shoot some asteroids or some alien space ships – enjoy watching them explode… When players finished a level or two they might just turn off the app and switch back on later. Background of the Game

This one started off as a little side project when I had just finished the basic features of my D&D-character-sheet-App, had cleaned the code and had written comments for publishing the code on GitHub. After a lot of commenting I felt the urge to do something more splashy.* Of course there are already thousands of space shooters out there. The concept is surely far from being a genuine new idea. But hey: space shooters are still fun anyway. To be honest, in the beginning that was the main the reason to make this little game. But, soon after starting the development process I realized this was a project within a practicable scope for a single person to finish in a reasonable amount of time. So I thought this would be probably a good way to demonstrate the skillset required to develop with Unity until the larger projects have progressed further on.

Have fun trying it out!

Farm of the Dead

Game Teaser

It is a bright day… You enjoy the morning sun tickling your nose while you tend to the fields after you already fed the animals at dawn. As the smell of the ripe tomatoes reaches your nose and you glance at the reflections of light dancing on the growing plants and blooming flowers and you think to yourself: ‚What a wonderful world!‘
Then a different scent creeps into your nostrils.
The revolting sweet stench of rotting flesh.
– You can already hear the buzzing of the bluebottles.
Seconds later a twiching thing of blue and green appears in the periphery of your sight.
There behind the fence…
One of them…
As you take a closer look, you can behold it in all its ghastly form…
Now you remember…
It is more than remarkable that you can actually forget – even for a short moment:
The world already ended…

About the App

Farm of the Dead is (planned as) a farming and a survival game at once – but with the focus on farming, building, crafting and gathering of resources: When the game begins the player assumes the role of a survivor of yet another zombie apocalypse. While fleeing from some of those gruesome fellows the players character stumbles upon an abandoned farm. What in this very moment is no more than a – hopefully – relatively safe shelter for the night quickly becomes a new home. As players take a look around they will realize there are not only – potentially dwindling – supplies left here but a lot of tools, seeds that could bear fruit, a well for fresh water and in the basement… even a generator for electricity. If they would repair some of the fences and install a few security barriers they could actually endure in this place – at least for a while. And there is the goal of the game: build a relatively safe home for yourself – and if you ever meet a living human being again – maybe even others…
